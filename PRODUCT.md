# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single person managing their own tasks. They're at a desk (or couch) with a keyboard, trying to organize their day, focus on meaningful work, and feel a sense of progress. The app is a personal productivity cockpit, not a team collaboration tool.

## Product Purpose

FlowForge turns a todo list into a personal productivity cockpit. It gives one person the tools to capture tasks, organize them by priority and category, focus with a Pomodoro timer, track progress with analytics, and undo any mistake with time-travel undo/redo. It exists to make the user feel capable and in control, not overwhelmed.

## Brand Personality

Warm, approachable, human. The app should feel like a well-designed notebook, not a database. It earns trust through quality and clarity, never through flashy effects or corporate jargon. The tone is quietly confident — it assumes the user is competent and just needs the right tool.

## Anti-references

Not AI-generic. Avoid glassmorphism, pastel everything, gradient text, overly rounded cards, and the "SaaS cream" default palette. This should not look like another AI-generated productivity template. No big illustrations, no gamification, no playful mascots. The beauty comes from craft — spacing, typography, color restraint — not decoration.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every UI element answers "what does this do?" without a manual. Labels are verbs. Feedback is immediate.
2. **Respect the user's attention.** The default view is calm and focused. Features like the Pomodoro timer and analytics are available on demand, not always on screen.
3. **Craft at every size.** The spacing, color, and typography are as considered on a 400px phone as on a 1400px desktop. Nothing feels bolted on.
4. **Forgiving by design.** Undo/redo, deferred deletion, auto-save — the app assumes the user will change their mind and makes that easy.
5. **One human, one tool.** Every feature serves a single person managing their own workflow. There's no team, no sharing, no permissions. The intimacy of a personal tool is a feature, not a limitation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (4.5:1 text contrast, keyboard navigable, visible focus indicators). Support prefers-reduced-motion. Support dark mode.
